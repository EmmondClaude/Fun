# Refracted Dark — an Afterdark design philosophy

*Movement name:* **Refracted Dark.** The visual worldview behind every Afterdark
build. Written as a manifesto so it can guide any expression — a hero, a poster, a
full site — without dictating layout.

## The philosophy

One light source, refracted. Everything you see is the same beam of white light
caught at a different angle — bent through glass until it splits into a spectrum.
Nothing in Refracted Dark is decoration for its own sake; every glow, every edge,
every shift of hue is the *same* light revealing another of its faces. The work
should feel inevitable, as though the spectrum was always inside the void and the
design merely turned it toward you.

**Space and form.** The canvas is a deep, near-black void — not flat black, but a
radial darkness that breathes from a soft center (`#141121`) out to true shadow
(`#07060B`). Forms float in this void with room around them; one object holds the
eye and everything else recedes. Negative space is not empty, it is *charged* —
the held breath before the light moves. Composition favors a single dominant
gesture surrounded by stillness, the way a single struck note rings clearest in
silence.

**Color and material.** Color arrives only as refracted light: cyan (`#22E6FF`),
its paler ghost (`#7FF0FF`), violet (`#8B5CF6`), magenta (`#FF2E97`), and its deep
ember (`#C70E6E`) — always in that order, always the same spectrum, so the whole
world reads as one prism seen from many sides. Materials are glass, not paint:
transmission, refraction, fresnel rim-light at the silhouette, the oil-slick
shimmer of iridescence. Light bends *through* things and picks up the spectrum
behind them. The surface is never matte; it is wet, lit, alive.

**Scale and rhythm.** Type is rare and deliberate — a serif display wordmark held
large and quiet, a single line of mono meta whispered beneath. Words are an accent
on the light, never an explanation of it. Motion has weight: nothing snaps, nothing
is linear; everything decelerates into place with the inertia of real mass. The
rhythm is patient — a slow idle drift, a reveal that *earns* its arrival.

**Composition and hierarchy.** There is always a lead and always a chorus. The eye
is told exactly where to go by light, not by arrows. The spectrum is the only
information system that matters: where the light is warmest, that is the point of
focus. This restraint inside abundance — much motion, one material, one light — is
the entire discipline. Five effects fighting each other is noise; one light shown
five ways is composition.

**Craft.** Refracted Dark must look meticulously crafted — the product of deep
expertise and painstaking attention, master-level execution in every easing curve
and every gradient stop. The neon is gorgeous but never garish; the glow is
controlled, thresholded, earned. It should read as though someone at the very top
of their field labored over the weight of each transition and the exact calibration
of each hue until the spectrum felt not designed but *discovered.* Anything less —
any uniform glow, any linear motion, any color that strays from the one true
spectrum — breaks the spell.

## The quiet reference

Woven invisibly through the work: a **prism** — five facets, the same five-sided
mark that is the Blaque mark. Those who know will feel the geometry of refraction
in the composition; everyone else simply experiences light bending through dark.

## How this expresses (for the build)

- Void radial background, never flat black.
- One reactive hero object in glass/refraction material; spectrum sampled at the
  fresnel rim so the silhouette glows in the brand ramp.
- A single large serif wordmark + one mono meta line. No paragraphs in the hero.
- Weighted, decelerating motion with idle drift; nothing linear.
- The whole thing must be beautiful when frozen — the still frame is the poster.
